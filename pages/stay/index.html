should i stay or i go now?


time is running out.
consistently,
the drip-drip-drip
of water against rock,
eroding a spot for itself.
the slow immersion of
land into sea,
as tendrils of saltwater
creep closer to habitation.

do you think the earth
spends moments wondering
about how it will cease to exist?
do you think the turtles
discuss amongst themselves,
coming up with theories
about the apocalypse?
do you think a draining life
spends its final days
worrying about the last breath?

even as you love,
why are you thinking about the end?
the split second where
you are bereft,
unmoored,
alone.

when you look back
on these moments,
it is not the warmth of
being held,
but the fear of losing
that rears its head.
and slowly, every memory
is tainted by the possibility of loss.

but loss does not lessen
the more you think about it.
there is no crash course,
no preparatory weeks
to absorb the impact.

when loss hits,
as it eventually must,
you will stumble.
you will feel as if
the earth has been ripped out
from under you.

but what tides you through,
are the memories of love.

to love and to lose,
they say.
for it is one that helps the other.

so if you are constantly
thinking about letting go,

why would you bother holding on at all?

.
